Open Computational Physiology Engine (OCPE)
===========================================

Vision Document
---------------

Mission
-------

The Open Computational Physiology Engine (OCPE) aims to create an open, extensible computational framework for modeling, simulating, and analyzing human physiology using multimodal data.

OCPE will provide the foundation for a new generation of digital physiology tools that bridge wearable sensing, computational modeling, artificial intelligence, and clinical research. The goal is to enable researchers, engineers, and clinicians to better understand complex physiological systems by transforming fragmented biological signals into meaningful models of human health.

The Problem
-----------

Modern healthcare is optimized around episodic measurements: laboratory tests, imaging studies, and clinical encounters that capture brief snapshots of a continuously changing biological system.

However, many chronic and systemic conditions are characterized by dynamic physiological dysfunction that may not be visible during traditional assessments.

Examples include:

-   Long COVID
-   Myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS)
-   Dysautonomia
-   Autoimmune disorders
-   Metabolic dysfunction
-   Chronic inflammatory conditions
-   Neurological disorders

These conditions often involve interactions across multiple physiological systems:

-   Cardiovascular regulation
-   Autonomic nervous system function
-   Immune response
-   Metabolism
-   Sleep and circadian rhythms
-   Activity and recovery
-   Stress response

Current tools lack a unified computational framework for representing these interactions over time.

Vision
------

OCPE envisions a future where human physiology can be represented as a dynamic computational system.

Instead of viewing health as isolated measurements, OCPE will enable modeling of:

-   Physiological states
-   System interactions
-   Disease progression
-   Recovery trajectories
-   Individual responses to interventions

The long-term vision is a computational "digital physiology layer" that allows researchers and clinicians to simulate, analyze, and better understand complex biological systems.

Core Principles
---------------

### 1\. Open Science First

OCPE will be built as an open computational platform that encourages collaboration, transparency, and reproducibility.

The project will prioritize:

-   Open-source software
-   Open standards
-   Reproducible research workflows
-   Community-driven development

### 2\. Physiology as a Dynamic System

Human physiology is not a collection of independent biomarkers. It is a complex adaptive system.

OCPE will model:

-   Temporal relationships
-   Feedback loops
-   Physiological constraints
-   Individual variability
-   Responses to perturbations

### 3\. Multimodal Data Integration

Physiological understanding requires combining multiple sources of information.

OCPE will support integration of:

-   Wearable sensors
-   Continuous physiological monitoring
-   Patient-reported outcomes
-   Clinical measurements
-   Laboratory results
-   Environmental and behavioral data

### 4\. AI-Augmented Physiology

Artificial intelligence will be used as a tool to discover patterns, generate hypotheses, and assist interpretation.

OCPE will explore:

-   Machine learning models
-   Foundation models for physiology
-   Representation learning
-   Anomaly detection
-   Personalized physiological modeling

AI will augment scientific understanding rather than replace physiological knowledge.

Initial Focus
-------------

The first version of OCPE will establish the computational foundation required for physiological modeling.

Initial capabilities include:

### Synthetic Physiology Generation

A synthetic physiology engine capable of generating realistic physiological time-series data for:

-   Algorithm development
-   Model validation
-   Research prototyping
-   Education

### Wearable Data Modeling

Frameworks for processing and analyzing wearable sensor streams including:

-   Heart rate
-   Heart rate variability
-   Activity
-   Sleep
-   Respiratory signals
-   Temperature
-   Other emerging biomarkers

### Physiological State Representation

Development of computational representations of physiological states, including:

-   Baseline states
-   Stress responses
-   Recovery states
-   Disease-associated patterns

### Research Infrastructure

Tools that allow researchers to:

-   Generate datasets
-   Evaluate algorithms
-   Compare models
-   Share computational physiology workflows

Long-Term Goals
---------------

The long-term goal of OCPE is to become the foundational computational infrastructure for personalized physiology.

Future capabilities may include:

### Digital Physiology Models

Individualized models that continuously learn from a person's physiological data.

### Disease Modeling

Computational models that help characterize diseases where traditional diagnostics fail to capture dynamic dysfunction.

### Intervention Simulation

Tools that allow researchers to explore:

-   Treatment responses
-   Lifestyle interventions
-   Rehabilitation strategies
-   Physiological adaptation

### Precision Neuromodulation

A future objective is enabling computational approaches to understand and optimize interventions targeting biological regulation, including neuromodulation approaches for systemic disorders.

Research Philosophy
-------------------

OCPE is built on the belief that many unsolved medical challenges require a shift from static measurement toward dynamic systems modeling.

The next generation of medicine will require:

-   Better physiological data
-   Better computational models
-   Better understanding of biological complexity

OCPE seeks to provide the infrastructure necessary to make that future possible.

Community
---------

OCPE is intended to serve:

-   Biomedical researchers
-   Data scientists
-   Engineers
-   Clinicians
-   Patients and advocates
-   Open-source contributors

By creating shared computational infrastructure, OCPE aims to accelerate discovery and improve our collective understanding of human physiology.

The Future
----------

The ultimate vision of OCPE is a world where physiology can be measured, modeled, and understood at a level of detail that enables earlier detection, better treatment, and more personalized healthcare.

Human biology is complex.

Our tools for understanding it should be equally sophisticated.
