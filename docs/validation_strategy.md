# Validation Strategy for Computational Physiology

This document outlines the multi-tiered validation strategy implemented in the open computational physiology engine to ensure scientific accuracy, clinical plausibility, and reproducibility of simulation outcomes.

---

## 1. Multi-Tiered Validation Framework

We establish four distinct levels of validation, executing from raw metadata to clinical output:

```text
Level 1: Schema & Semantic Validation (Programmatic)
  │   - YAML format syntax validation
  │   - Variable symbol cross-referencing and registry lookup
  │   - Units and LaTeX equation checks
  ▼
Level 2: Physiological Plausibility Validation (Simulation Boundary Checks)
  │   - Verify state variables stay within extreme boundaries (e.g. SBP [50, 200] mmHg)
  │   - Check conservation of mass (volumes add up to Total Volume)
  │   - Inspect signal ranges for overflow/underflow
  ▼
Level 3: Clinical Cohort Diagnostic Validation (Physiological Response Check)
  │   - Compare HR and BP changes during stress challenges against published criteria
  │   - POTS: HR rise >= 30 bpm on standing
  │   - Healthy: HR rise < 30 bpm on standing (with muscle pump caveats)
  ▼
Level 4: Wearable Sensor Agreement Validation (Statistical Agreement)
      - Bland-Altman limit of agreement (LoA) checks
      - Intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC) evaluations
      - Pearson's correlation coefficient checks vs. gold standard ECG
```

---

## 2. Sensor Validation Standards

Wearable sensors generated by the model must comply with validation criteria extracted from clinical studies (e.g., Schaffarczyk 2022):

1. **Intraclass Correlation Coefficient (ICC)**: Measures the absolute agreement of wearable heart rate variability (HRV) metrics against clinical ECG.
   * *Target*: $\text{ICC} \ge 0.95$ for resting conditions, and $\text{ICC} \ge 0.85$ for movement transitions.
2. **Concordance Correlation Coefficient (CCC)**: Assesses the precision and accuracy of wearable pulse rate variability (PRV) derived from optical PPG.
3. **Bland-Altman Limits of Agreement (LoA)**: Ensures the bias (mean difference) and limits of agreement (95% difference bounds) are within acceptable clinical limits.
   * *Target*: Bias $< 1.0$ ms, $\text{LoA} \le \pm 20.0$ ms for inter-beat intervals.

## 3. Current Model Limitations & Evolutionary Direction

During Head-Up Tilt (HUT) test simulations, our Physiological Evaluator flagged a validation failure for the Healthy Control cohort:
* **The Discrepancy**: The nominal "healthy" patient exhibited an HR rise of **76.1 bpm**, violating the clinical criterion of $\Delta \text{HR} < 30$ bpm.
* **The Cause**: The Geddes 2022 model is a zero-dimensional lumped parameter model that does **not** include an active skeletal muscle pump. In real life, skeletal muscle contraction in the legs actively pumps pooled venous blood back to the heart, mitigating the drop in stroke volume and maintaining pressure. Without this pump, the model pools blood excessively, requiring a massive compensatory tachycardia to prevent circulatory collapse.
* **Evolutionary Plan**:
  1. **Incorporate Muscle Pump**: Update the mathematical equations in future versions to include a time-varying lower venous capacity ($VMvl(t)$) or active venous squeeze parameter ($Q_{pump}$) triggered during posture transition.
  2. **Introduce Circadian Modulation**: Integrate circadian drive ($D_{circ}$) and sleep pressure ($P_sleep$) as parameters perturbing the baseline baroreflex gains ($k_R, k_H$) to simulate diurnal variations in orthostatic tolerance.
  3. **Patient-Specific Calibration**: Implement Kalman filters to fit nominal parameters ($Cau, Cal$) to real-world resting heart rate and blood pressure streams from wearable sensors.
